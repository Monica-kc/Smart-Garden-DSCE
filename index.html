INTRODUCTION

A smart garden is a system that monitors the environment of plants. Maintaining a garden is a time-consuming and laborious effort in and of itself; on top of that, watering plants with minimal waste has become a must to reduce energy usage and conserve water. The same technology that allows us to live comfortably within our own four walls can also help us build our own private oasis of natural nourishment. This proposal proposes a smart gardening system that makes use of the Internet of Things concept (IoT). The major goal of this project is to reduce water consumption when gardening and to maintain the garden remotely. Important plant data, such as temperature, relative humidity, and soil moisture, is continuously stored in a cloud-based database in this system. Artificial Intelligence (AI)-based planning is carried out on a regular basis to water the plants and give appropriate lighting in the garden area for aesthetic purposes. The Blynk application is used to view sensor data from a remote location. Notification service can also be introduced with the help of the Blynk app.

The main goal is to conserve water and resources while maximizing solar energy usage.

VISION

The Smart Garden project intends to create a product-service system with sophisticated technological and social capabilities that can govern major components of a public or private garden using the Internet of Things. Thanks to the development of a multi-sensor system and a cloud platform for managing the collected data, it will be possible to intervene on factors such as the health of the garden and the people who visit it, air quality, irrigation, lighting, atmospheric and sound pollution, and the overall sustainability of the system.
